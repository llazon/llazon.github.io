# About Me

Hey there! I'm an infrastructure and cloud engineer who lives and breathes AWS. I spend my days building, securing, and scaling cloud infrastructure — and most nights reading about it too.

I've been an AWS Community Builder since 2022, and this blog is where I share the things I learn along the way. No fluff, just real-world notes from the trenches of running production workloads on AWS.

## What I Do

I work across the full AWS stack — from IAM policies and networking to serverless architectures and observability pipelines. My day-to-day involves a mix of:

- Infrastructure as Code (Terraform, CloudFormation)
- Security hardening and compliance (GuardDuty, SCPs, SOC 2)
- Monitoring and observability (Grafana, Prometheus, CloudWatch)
- Automation with Lambda, SSM, and a healthy amount of shell scripting
- Cost optimization and architecture reviews
- The occasional Rust project when the mood strikes

## About This Blog

"All Things AWS" started as a place to document solutions to problems I kept running into. It turns out writing things down is the best way to actually remember them — and if it helps someone else avoid the same rabbit holes, even better.

Topics tend to follow whatever I'm working on at the time, which means you'll find everything from load balancer edge cases to GPU instance quirks to re:Invent recaps.

## Community

Being part of the AWS Community Builder program has been a great way to connect with other builders, get early access to new services, and stay sharp. If you're thinking about applying, do it — the community is genuinely welcoming and the conversations are worth it.
