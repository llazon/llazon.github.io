# AWS re:Invent 2025: Highlights and Takeaways

Another year, another re:Invent. Las Vegas in December is its own kind of experience — 50,000+ cloud enthusiasts, a week of keynotes, sessions, and hallway conversations that somehow always end up being the most valuable part. Here's my roundup of what stood out this year.

## The Big Themes

### AI Infrastructure Goes Mainstream

This was the year AI stopped being a separate track and became part of everything. The keynotes leaned heavily into how AWS is building AI capabilities directly into existing services rather than requiring teams to adopt entirely new tools:

- Bedrock integrations showing up in services you wouldn't expect
- CodeWhisperer evolution into a broader development assistant
- SageMaker simplifications that lower the barrier for teams without dedicated ML engineers
- New instance types optimized for inference workloads at lower price points

The message was clear: AI should be a feature of your existing workflow, not a separate project.

### Security as a Platform

The Security Hub consolidation I wrote about in November was a big talking point. AWS doubled down on the unified security story:

- New automated remediation capabilities
- Cross-service correlation getting smarter
- Zero-trust networking patterns becoming first-class
- Identity-based security controls expanding

### Sustainability and Efficiency

AWS continued pushing the sustainability angle, but this year it felt more practical:

- New Graviton4 instances with better performance per watt
- Carbon-aware scheduling options for batch workloads
- Better visibility into the environmental impact of your infrastructure choices
- Data center efficiency improvements that translate to cost savings

## Sessions That Stood Out

### "Multi-Account Security at Scale"

This session validated a lot of the SCP patterns I wrote about in January. The presenter walked through a real-world implementation managing 500+ accounts with Organizations and SCPs. Key takeaway: the OU structure matters more than the individual policies.

### "Cost Optimization Beyond the Basics"

Went beyond the usual "right-size your instances" advice. Covered data transfer cost patterns that most people overlook — inter-AZ traffic, NAT Gateway processing, and cross-region replication costs. The VPC endpoint math I covered in July came up almost verbatim.

### "Rust in Production on AWS"

A session after my own heart. Teams shared their experience running Rust Lambda functions in production, including the cold start benchmarks and operational patterns. Nice to see the Rust-on-Lambda ecosystem maturing.

### "Incident Response with AWS Native Tools"

CloudTrail + Athena + Security Hub for building incident response playbooks. Very aligned with the approach I covered in my August post. The addition of automated timeline generation was new and impressive.

## Announcements Worth Watching

Without going into every announcement (there were hundreds), here are the ones I think will matter most for day-to-day infrastructure work:

1. **Enhanced Savings Plans flexibility** — easier to modify commitments mid-term
2. **S3 Express One Zone improvements** — lower latency for high-performance workloads
3. **ECS improvements** — better integration with service mesh and observability
4. **CloudFormation and Terraform parity** — AWS continuing to invest in IaC tooling
5. **New regions and Local Zones** — expanding the global footprint

## The Community Builder Experience

Being at re:Invent as a Community Builder has its perks — dedicated lounge, early access to some sessions, and meetups with other builders. The conversations with people solving similar problems in different industries are consistently the highlight.

This year's Community Builder meetup had a great discussion about content creation cadence and how to balance writing with actual engineering work. Spoiler: nobody has it figured out, but everyone agreed that writing about what you're already doing (rather than inventing topics) is the sustainable approach.

## Hallway Track Highlights

Some of the best conversations happened between sessions:

- Talked with a team running 10,000+ Lambda functions in production — their observability approach was fascinating
- Met someone who automated their entire compliance audit with Security Hub and custom controls
- Had a great debate about Terraform vs CDK vs CloudFormation (no consensus reached, as usual)
- Connected with other Community Builders working on similar infrastructure challenges

## Looking Ahead to 2026

Based on the announcements and the general direction, here's what I'm planning to dig into:

- The new Security Hub features for our compliance workflows
- Graviton4 migration for our compute fleet
- Expanded use of Rust for performance-critical Lambda functions
- Better cost visibility with the new Cost Explorer features

re:Invent is exhausting and overwhelming and absolutely worth it. If you've been on the fence about attending, go. The sessions are great, but the people you meet and the conversations you have are what make it special.

See you in Vegas next year.
